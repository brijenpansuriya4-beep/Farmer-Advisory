Kerala Government Agriculture Schemes

Empowering Farmers with Sustainable Solutions

1. Organic Farming Mission

Established in November 2023, this mission aims to expand organic cultivation across 5,000 hectares over five years, with a goal of 1,000 hectares annually. It includes certification, branding, value addition, and marketing support through farmer groups like Krishikoottam and Kudumbashree.

Source: The Hindu

2. Haritha Keralam Mission

A holistic public mission focused on organic agriculture, soil and water conservation, waste management, and ecological restoration. Initiatives include barren land transformation, vertical farming, medicinal plant cultivation, and watershed-based planning.

Source: haritham.kerala.gov.in

3. Small Farmers Agri-Business Consortium (FPO Policy)

Started in May 2020, this policy aims to establish and strengthen 50 Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs), helping farmers with aggregation, value chain development, and better marketing outcomes.

Source: sfackerala.org

4. Polam Pilusthondi & Chandranna Rythu Kshetralu

Polam Pilusthondi provides direct services to farmers like soil health testing, zero-budget farming, and drought mitigation. Chandranna Rythu Kshetralu promotes modern farming by offering ‚Çπ5,000 per hectare as a subsidy for adopting advanced practices.

Source: Kerala PCS Exam Notes

5. Farmers Service Centre (FSC)

FSCs act as local hubs that provide farmers with information on loans, subsidies, credits, mechanization, and support for organic or bio-pharmacy-based setups at the block level.

Source: cooperation.kerala.gov.in

6. Kerala Farmers' Welfare Fund (Welfare Nidhi)

This fund, launched in 2019, provides welfare benefits to small and marginal farmers. It covers cultivation support, allied activities, pension benefits, and encourages youth participation in farming, benefiting over 20 lakh farmers.

Source: kfwfb.kerala.gov.in
